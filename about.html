Things To Do

Fun places and activities to incorporate into your next visit!

The Grand Canyon offers a plethora of exciting activities for visitors of all ages. Here are some fun things you can do:

    Hiking: With numerous trails catering to various skill levels, hiking is one of the best ways to experience the Grand Canyon. Popular trails include the Bright Angel Trail, South Kaibab Trail, and Rim Trail.
    Scenic Drives: Enjoy breathtaking views of the Grand Canyon from the comfort of your car by driving along the scenic Desert View Drive or Hermit Road. Don't forget to stop at various overlooks for photo opportunities.
    Sunrise/Sunset Viewing: Witnessing sunrise or sunset over the Grand Canyon is a must-do experience. The changing colors of the canyon as the sun rises or sets create a truly mesmerizing sight.
    Visitor Centers and Museums: Learn about the geology, history, and wildlife of the Grand Canyon by visiting one of its many visitor centers and museums. These educational facilities offer interactive exhibits and ranger-led programs.
    Camping: Spend a night under the stars by camping at one of the Grand Canyon's campgrounds. Whether you prefer developed campsites or backcountry camping, camping allows you to fully immerse yourself in the canyon's natural beauty.
    Photography: Bring your camera or smartphone and capture the stunning vistas, rock formations, and wildlife that abound in the Grand Canyon. Photography enthusiasts will find endless opportunities for unique shots.
    Helicopter Tours: For a bird's-eye view of the Grand Canyon, consider taking a helicopter tour. These tours provide an exhilarating way to see the canyon's vastness and scale from above.
